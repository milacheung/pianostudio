# MERIDIAN MUSIC BOARD — PIANO SYLLABUS (FICTIONAL SAMPLE)
# All pieces, composers, and requirements are invented for demonstration purposes.

## GRADE 1

### Pieces (choose one from each list)

**List A — Classical**
- "Morning Walk" by C. Hartwell (develops even finger weight, simple legato)
- "Country Dance" by A. Merritt (develops rhythmic accuracy, staccato touch)
- "Silver Brook" by T. Lansing (develops right-hand melody with left-hand accompaniment)

**List B — Romantic**
- "Little Lullaby" by M. Fontaine (develops smooth legato, dynamic contrast)
- "March of the Soldiers" by D. Whittaker (develops firm staccato, clear articulation)

**List C — Contemporary**
- "Raindrops" by S. Chen (develops independent hand movement, soft dynamics)
- "The Robot's Dance" by K. Abara (develops rhythmic precision, hand coordination)

### Scales and Arpeggios
- C major and A minor (natural): hands separately, one octave
- C major: contrary motion, one octave
- Speed: approximately MM=50-60

### Sight-Reading
- Candidates will be asked to play a short piece of approximately 8 bars in 4/4 or 3/4 time, not exceeding 5-finger position. The examiner will allow 30 seconds of preparation time.

### Aural Tests
- Clap the pulse of a short piece played twice by the examiner
- Identify whether a short phrase moves up or down

## GRADE 2

### Pieces (choose one from each list)

**List A — Classical**
- "Minuet in D" by L. Voss (develops left-hand independence, Baroque style)
- "Sonatina Theme" by P. Crane (develops left-hand independence with melodic bass line)
- "Gavotte" by R. Aldrich (develops two-voice texture, ornaments)

**List B — Romantic**
- "Evening Song" by M. Fontaine (develops singing tone, expressive dynamics)
- "Spinning Wheel" by A. Merritt (develops left-hand accompaniment patterns, coordination)
- "Waltz in Blue" by T. Lansing (develops left-hand waltz pattern, phrasing)

**List C — Contemporary**
- "Cloud Patterns" by S. Chen (develops independent hand movement, soft pedaling)
- "Market Day" by K. Abara (develops rhythmic energy, staccato bass)

### Scales and Arpeggios
- G major and E minor (natural and harmonic): hands separately and together, one octave
- C major: hands together, one octave
- Speed: approximately MM=60-72

### Sight-Reading
- A short piece of approximately 8-12 bars, simple key signature (up to 1 sharp or flat), including basic rhythmic patterns. Preparation time: 30 seconds.

### Aural Tests
- Clap back a short rhythm (4 bars) played twice by the examiner
- Identify whether a cadence is complete (perfect) or incomplete (imperfect)
- Sing back a short 4-bar phrase after hearing it twice

## GRADE 3

### Pieces (choose one from each list)

**List A — Classical**
- "Sonata Fragment in C" by P. Crane (develops two-hand independence, Classical structure)
- "Allemande" by R. Aldrich (develops finger independence, Baroque ornamentation)
- "Theme and Variation" by L. Voss (develops technique variety, dynamic contrast)

**List B — Romantic**
- "Nocturne Sketch" by M. Fontaine (develops singing tone, rubato, pedaling)
- "Etude in A minor" by T. Lansing (develops right-hand evenness, left-hand chord support)
- "Ballade" by A. Merritt (develops narrative phrasing, rhythmic flexibility)

**List C — Contemporary**
- "City Lights" by S. Chen (develops pedaling, atmospheric dynamics)
- "Perpetual Motion" by K. Abara (develops finger evenness, stamina)

### Scales and Arpeggios
- D major, B minor (natural and harmonic), F major, D minor (natural and harmonic): hands together, one octave
- Chromatic scale starting on C: hands separately
- Arpeggios: C major and A minor, hands separately
- Speed: approximately MM=66-80

### Sight-Reading
- A piece of approximately 12-16 bars in keys up to 2 sharps or flats. Must include at least one position shift. Preparation time: 30 seconds.

### Aural Tests
- Clap back a 4-bar rhythm including dotted note patterns
- Identify the key of a short piece (major or minor)
- Sing back a 4-bar melody after hearing it twice
- Describe the mood and dynamics of a short piece played by the examiner
